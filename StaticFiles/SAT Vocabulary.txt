<p>A lot of students immediately think of vocabulary when they hear the word SAT. But are SAT vocab words really that important for doing well on the test? Kind of. <strong>If you’re aiming for a high score, you’ll definitely want to spend some time learning key SAT words.</strong></p>
<p>In this guide, we give you a comprehensive list of 262 of the most common SAT vocabulary words. We also explain how vocab is tested on the SAT, what types of questions you’ll see, and how to get the most out of your vocab prep.</p>
<p><!--more--></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>How Is Vocabulary Tested on the SAT?</h2>
<p>The SAT underwent <a href="/complete-guide-to-the-new-sat-in-2016" target="_blank" rel="noopener">a significant redesign in 2016</a>, and since then vocabulary has become a <a href="/how-important-is-vocabulary-for-the-new-sat" target="_blank" rel="noopener">far less important</a> part of the test. This is because there are fewer vocabulary questions on the current SAT than there were on the old SAT.&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">That said, </span>it'll still benefit you to study vocab,<span style="background-color: transparent;"> especially if you're aiming for a high or </span><a href="/how-to-get-a-perfect-sat-score-by-a-2400-sat-scorer" target="_blank" style="background-color: transparent;" rel="noopener">perfect score</a><span style="background-color: transparent;">.</span></p>
<p>All SAT words are about <strong>medium difficulty&nbsp;</strong>and are&nbsp;<strong>tested in the context of reading passages,</strong> so you’ll get not just a sentence but an entire paragraph or passage to work with.</p>
<p>Context clues make memorizing SAT vocab words less important on the current SAT than it was on the old SAT, back when you had to memorize dozens of obscure words and answer questions that dealt with isolated sentences (<a href="/new-sat-in-2016-reading-section-changes-strategies" target="_blank" rel="noopener">these were called Sentence Completion problems</a>).&nbsp;<span style="background-color: transparent;">This made vocab questions particularly hard since you were given minimal context with which to solve them.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>What Do SAT Vocabulary Questions Look Like?</h2>
<p>Purely vocabulary-based questions don’t make up a large part of the SAT. <span style="background-color: transparent;">Based on our analysis of </span><a href="/complete-official-sat-practice-tests-free-links" target="_blank" style="background-color: transparent;" rel="noopener">official SAT practice tests</a><span style="background-color: transparent;">,</span><strong style="background-color: transparent;">&nbsp;13% of the Reading section (or about seven questions) ask you to match a word with its correct meaning.&nbsp;</strong><span>We call these&nbsp;</span><strong>Words in Context&nbsp;</strong>questions.&nbsp;<span style="background-color: transparent;"></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">For these questions, you'll need to know alternative meanings of relatively common words, such as “directly” and “hold.” While you might</span><span style="background-color: transparent;">&nbsp;come across slightly more difficult SAT words, you generally shouldn’t see any as hard as those on the old SAT.</span></p>

<h2>How to Study SAT Words Effectively: 3 Essential Tips</h2>
<p>Now that you've got a huge list of SAT vocabulary words you can work with, what’s the best way to study them? Here are three key tips to help you get the most out of your SAT vocab studies.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3><span style="background-color: transparent;">#1: Make Flashcards and Use the Waterfall Method</span></h3>
<p>One of the absolute best ways to study SAT vocab words is to <strong>make flashcards.&nbsp;</strong><span style="background-color: transparent;">This lets you control which SAT words you study and even randomizes them so that you don't accidentally memorize words in a predetermined order.</span></p>
<p>We recommend using the <strong>waterfall method</strong>&nbsp;to study your flashcards.<strong>&nbsp;</strong><span style="background-color: transparent;">With this method, you'll get to see all the words in your deck, going over the most challenging words more often than the ones you already know or sort of know.</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">Here's how to use the waterfall method:</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;"><img src="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220415.jpg?width=726&amp;height=226&amp;name=file-2231220415.jpg" alt="body_vocab1.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="726" height="226" srcset="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220415.jpg?width=363&amp;height=113&amp;name=file-2231220415.jpg 363w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220415.jpg?width=726&amp;height=226&amp;name=file-2231220415.jpg 726w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220415.jpg?width=1089&amp;height=339&amp;name=file-2231220415.jpg 1089w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220415.jpg?width=1452&amp;height=452&amp;name=file-2231220415.jpg 1452w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220415.jpg?width=1815&amp;height=565&amp;name=file-2231220415.jpg 1815w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220415.jpg?width=2178&amp;height=678&amp;name=file-2231220415.jpg 2178w" sizes="(max-width: 726px) 100vw, 726px"></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">Once you’ve made your flashcards, split them up into decks (you can put whatever words you want in these decks) of about <strong>30-50 cards each.</strong> Choose one deck to be your <strong>Starting Stack.&nbsp;</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">Go through your Starting Stack, looking at each and every card. For the words you know, put them in a <strong>Know It</strong> pile. For the words you <em>don’t</em> know, put them in a separate <strong>Struggled</strong> pile as so:</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;"><img src="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220455.jpg?width=707&amp;height=222&amp;name=file-2231220455.jpg" alt="body_vocab2.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="707" height="222" srcset="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220455.jpg?width=354&amp;height=111&amp;name=file-2231220455.jpg 354w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220455.jpg?width=707&amp;height=222&amp;name=file-2231220455.jpg 707w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220455.jpg?width=1061&amp;height=333&amp;name=file-2231220455.jpg 1061w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220455.jpg?width=1414&amp;height=444&amp;name=file-2231220455.jpg 1414w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220455.jpg?width=1768&amp;height=555&amp;name=file-2231220455.jpg 1768w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220455.jpg?width=2121&amp;height=666&amp;name=file-2231220455.jpg 2121w" sizes="(max-width: 707px) 100vw, 707px"></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">Now, pick up your Struggled pile and go through each card in it (leave your Know It pile where it is). Put the cards you know in a second Know It pile and the ones you don’t know in a Struggled pile.</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">You should now have two Know It piles and one Struggled pile:</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;"><img src="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220420.jpg?width=700&amp;height=217&amp;name=file-2231220420.jpg" alt="body_vocab3.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="700" height="217" srcset="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220420.jpg?width=350&amp;height=109&amp;name=file-2231220420.jpg 350w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220420.jpg?width=700&amp;height=217&amp;name=file-2231220420.jpg 700w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220420.jpg?width=1050&amp;height=326&amp;name=file-2231220420.jpg 1050w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220420.jpg?width=1400&amp;height=434&amp;name=file-2231220420.jpg 1400w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220420.jpg?width=1750&amp;height=543&amp;name=file-2231220420.jpg 1750w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220420.jpg?width=2100&amp;height=651&amp;name=file-2231220420.jpg 2100w" sizes="(max-width: 700px) 100vw, 700px"></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">Repeat this process of picking up your Struggled pile and going through each card until you’re left with about <strong>one to five cards in your Struggled pile:</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;"><img src="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220425.jpg?width=706&amp;height=218&amp;name=file-2231220425.jpg" alt="body_vocab4.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="706" height="218" srcset="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220425.jpg?width=353&amp;height=109&amp;name=file-2231220425.jpg 353w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220425.jpg?width=706&amp;height=218&amp;name=file-2231220425.jpg 706w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220425.jpg?width=1059&amp;height=327&amp;name=file-2231220425.jpg 1059w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220425.jpg?width=1412&amp;height=436&amp;name=file-2231220425.jpg 1412w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220425.jpg?width=1765&amp;height=545&amp;name=file-2231220425.jpg 1765w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220425.jpg?width=2118&amp;height=654&amp;name=file-2231220425.jpg 2118w" sizes="(max-width: 706px) 100vw, 706px"></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">By now you should know most, if not all, cards in your deck.&nbsp;</span><span style="background-color: transparent;">But it isn’t enough to just go through them once—<strong>you also have to work back up your “waterfall” of cards.</strong>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">To do this, combine your Struggled pile with your last Know It pile (the pile closest to the Struggled pile). T</span><span style="background-color: transparent;">his will be your <strong>Working</strong> pile. Go through all the cards in this pile. If there are any words you forgot, go through the entire pile again and again until you've learned <em>all</em> the definitions in it.</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;"><img src="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220465.jpg?width=717&amp;height=284&amp;name=file-2231220465.jpg" alt="body_vocab5.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="717" height="284" srcset="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220465.jpg?width=359&amp;height=142&amp;name=file-2231220465.jpg 359w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220465.jpg?width=717&amp;height=284&amp;name=file-2231220465.jpg 717w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220465.jpg?width=1076&amp;height=426&amp;name=file-2231220465.jpg 1076w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220465.jpg?width=1434&amp;height=568&amp;name=file-2231220465.jpg 1434w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220465.jpg?width=1793&amp;height=710&amp;name=file-2231220465.jpg 1793w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220465.jpg?width=2151&amp;height=852&amp;name=file-2231220465.jpg 2151w" sizes="(max-width: 717px) 100vw, 717px"></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">Continue this pattern by combining your current Working pile with the next Know It pile. Go through all these cards until you know each one.</span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;"><img src="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220460.jpg?width=711&amp;height=297&amp;name=file-2231220460.jpg" alt="body_vocab6.jpg" width="711" height="297" srcset="https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220460.jpg?width=356&amp;height=149&amp;name=file-2231220460.jpg 356w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220460.jpg?width=711&amp;height=297&amp;name=file-2231220460.jpg 711w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220460.jpg?width=1067&amp;height=446&amp;name=file-2231220460.jpg 1067w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220460.jpg?width=1422&amp;height=594&amp;name=file-2231220460.jpg 1422w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220460.jpg?width=1778&amp;height=743&amp;name=file-2231220460.jpg 1778w, https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/360031/file-2231220460.jpg?width=2133&amp;height=891&amp;name=file-2231220460.jpg 2133w" sizes="(max-width: 711px) 100vw, 711px"></span></p>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent;">At the end, you should have made your way all the back to your original Starting Stack. <strong>You now know all the SAT words and their meanings in your deck!&nbsp;</strong></span><span style="background-color: transparent;">Repeat this waterfall method with the other decks you make so that you can learn even more critical SAT vocab words.</span></p>
<h3>&nbsp;</h3>
<h3>#2: Focus On Words You Don’t Know</h3>
<p>If you don’t have time to study the entire list above or only want to learn some SAT words, use our list to <strong>make a new vocab list containing <em>only</em> the words you don’t know. </strong>So i<span style="background-color: transparent;">f you know a word or are fairly sure you’ll be able to recognize it on test day, skip it and instead focus on&nbsp;</span><span style="background-color: transparent;">the words you’ll have the most trouble remembering.&nbsp;</span><span style="background-color: transparent;">Once you have your list, use the waterfall method to study it.</span></p>
<p>If you’re not a fan of paper flashcards, you can opt for <strong>digital flashcards.</strong> <a href="https://apps.ankiweb.net/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Anki</a> is a free software you can download and use to make your own flashcards. The program uses spaced-repetition software (SRS) to show you difficult cards more often than those you know (basically, a digital version of the waterfall method).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>#3: Take Official SAT Practice Tests</h3>
<p>Since the majority of the words in our list above come from&nbsp;<a href="/complete-official-sat-practice-tests-free-links" target="_blank" rel="noopener">official SAT practice tests</a>, once you’ve studied these SAT words, you can put your knowledge to the test by taking a practice test.&nbsp;<span style="background-color: transparent;">This lets&nbsp;</span><span style="background-color: transparent;">you see whether you truly know the meanings of the words and&nbsp;<strong>whether you’ll be able to get the right answers in the context of a full-length, timed test—just like the real SAT.</strong></span></p>
<p>If you miss any questions because you forgot the meaning of a word, go back over our SAT vocab words list using your flashcards and the waterfall method.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>The Best SAT Vocab Flashcards</h2>
<p>If you plan to make your own SAT vocab flashcards from our list, you'll need&nbsp;at least 300 blank index cards and a system to keep them organized. These <a href="https://www.amazon.com/AmazonBasics-Heavy-Weight-3x5-Inch-300-Count/dp/B077K8JVW4/?tag=ps-ge5-20" rel="noopener" target="_blank">basic cards</a> are an affordable option that are <a href="https://www.amazon.com/AmazonBasics-Ruled-Assorted-3x5-Inch-300-Count/dp/B06XSXXKFZ/?tag=ps-ge5-20" rel="noopener" target="_blank">also available in&nbsp;fun colors</a>. You can keep them organized with plastic baggies or rubber bands, or you can get an <a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00H3E9FNO/?tag=ps-ge5-20" rel="noopener" target="_blank">organizer</a>. Alternatively, try&nbsp;these<a href="https://www.amazon.com/Koogel-Multicolor-Scratch-Bookmark-Greeting/dp/B074VYYWNW/?tag=ps-ge5-20" rel="noopener" target="_blank"> easy-flip flashcards</a>&nbsp;that&nbsp;include binder clips.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Though we strongly recommend making your own flashcards, you can also buy pre-made ones. There aren't a lot of options for the new SAT. We'd recommend going with&nbsp;Barron's&nbsp;<a href="https://www.amazon.com/1100-Words-Need-Know-Flashcards/dp/143807526X/?tag=ps-ge5-20" rel="noopener" target="_blank">1100 Words You Need to Know</a>, a series of exercises to master key words and&nbsp;idioms, or Manhattan's&nbsp;<a href="https://www.amazon.com/500-Advanced-Words-Vocabulary-Manhattan/dp/1935707884/?tag=ps-ge5-20">GRE flashcards</a> if you're looking for a challenge.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Conclusion: The Importance of Studying SAT Vocabulary</h2>
<p>Overall, vocabulary words don't play a huge part on the SAT. That said, you’ll definitely have some questions on both the Reading and Writing sections that test your knowledge of SAT words, so <strong>it’s important to study those most likely to appear on test day.</strong></p>
<p>The best way to study SAT vocabulary is to make flashcards and use the waterfall method. This ensures you know all the words in your deck and aren’t glossing over any difficult ones.</p>
<p>If you don’t want to make flashcards or don’t have time to study the whole list, however, it’s a good idea to pick out the words you don’t know and study just those. With these, you can either make a smaller deck of paper flashcards or opt for digital flashcards.</p>
<p>No matter how you choose to study SAT vocab words, be sure to&nbsp;<strong>test out what you’ve learned in the context of full-length practice tests.</strong> Nearly all the words in our list above come from official SAT practice tests, so you’re guaranteed to come across them in some form!</p>
